NBA teams scored an average of 114.6 points per game this season, the highest mark since the NBA-ABA merger in 1976.

While many factors contribute to the scoring trend, a big one is this: The league’s elite players are scoring more than ever. The top 20% of NBA players combined to average a record 22.8 points per game this year.

That record also makes for the largest-ever scoring gap between the top group of players and the bottom group of players.

The top players’ scoring outburst is fueled in part by the ongoing NBA love affair with three-point shots. The top 20% of players hit more threes this year than any other season in history.

All that scoring has not come at the cost of creating plays for teammates. At nearly five assists/game, top scorers are creating shots for teammates at a record level.
